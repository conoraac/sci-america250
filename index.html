RS
Dr. Robert T. SicoraFounder, Sicora Consulting · Leadership, Engagement & Culture
Most teams don't struggle because they lack talent. They struggle because they lack understanding of one another. I've sat with a lot of leaders who were convinced their team was "basically fine." On the surface, it was. People were polite. Deadlines mostly got hit. But underneath, something was off. Communication was guarded, the same conflicts kept resurfacing, and good people were quietly checking out. Here's the part most people miss: that isn't a character problem. It's a difference problem. We extend trust based on what matters to us, while the people around us are evaluating trust through a completely different lens. A Red builds it through results. A Yellow through connection. A Green through consistency. A Blue through accuracy. Same team, four different definitions of "trustworthy," and no shared language to bridge them. When a team finally gets that language, the conversation changes. People stop asking "what's wrong with them?" and start asking "what am I missing?" That single shift is where alignment, engagement, and momentum come from. This is the work we do: the 4 Colors™ of Insights™ to understand how people are wired, and the 8 Factors of Engagement® to understand what keeps them committed. To mark America's 250th, we've put both together in one place: our "250 for 250" experience: both assessments, both e-learning paths, and a live 30-minute coaching debrief for $250 (a $510 value), through July 31. If your team looks fine on the surface but you suspect there's more underneath, this is a good month to find out. The strongest teams don't eliminate their differences. They learn how to leverage them.
